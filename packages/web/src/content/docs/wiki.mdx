The **wiki** is the readable, organized output of your knowledge base. Every resource you ingest gets processed and placed into structured wiki pages — one per category — that you can open, read, and navigate directly from the app.

---

## What the wiki is

The wiki is a set of markdown pages, one per category (and one per subcategory), automatically generated from the content you've added. Each wiki page organizes the extracted knowledge under sections like `key-concepts`, `use-cases`, `tools`, and `resources`.

You never write wiki pages manually. Supadense builds them for you as you ingest resources.

---

## Opening the wiki

Click **Open Wiki** in the session header to open the wiki viewer. It opens in your browser and shows all categories currently in your KB.

You can also navigate directly to a category's wiki page from the chat by asking:

```
Open the wiki for Agents
Show me the RAG wiki
```

---

## Navigating the wiki

### Category sidebar

The left panel lists every category in your workspace. Click any category to open its wiki page.

### Section tabs

Each category page has tabs for the sections defined in that category:

- **key-concepts** — the main extracted knowledge, organized by concept or group
- **use-cases** — practical applications and example patterns
- **tools** — libraries, frameworks, and services mentioned
- **resources** — links and titles of the ingested resources

Click a tab to jump to that section.

### Sources panel

The right sidebar shows the **Sources** panel — a list of every resource that contributed content to the current category. Each entry shows:

- The resource title and URL
- Which sections it contributed to
- How many concepts were extracted from it

Click any source to open the original URL.

### Images panel

Also in the right sidebar, the **Images** panel shows all visuals extracted from resources in the current category — architecture diagrams, benchmark charts, paper figures. Click any image to expand it.

## How KB content becomes wiki pages

When you run `/memorize <url>`, the ingestion pipeline:

1. **Fetches and extracts** raw content from the URL (article text, YouTube transcript, paper PDF)
2. **Runs the AI pipeline** — extracts key concepts, use-cases, tools, and resources, mapped to your category's sections
3. **Creates placements** — each extracted item is stored as a placement linking the resource to a section in a category
4. **Builds the wiki** — `WikiBuilder` reads all placements for a category, renders them into markdown, and writes the wiki page to `wiki/<category>.md` in your KB directory

Every time you add or remove a resource, the affected category's wiki page is rebuilt automatically.

### Groups and the wiki

When you run `/group <category>`, Supadense re-renders the `key-concepts` section by clustering all placements into semantic groups. The wiki page is rebuilt to show concepts organized by group name rather than by resource.

See [Groups](/docs/groups) for details.

---

## Wiki files on disk

Wiki pages live at:

```
<workspace>/wiki/<category-slug>.md
```

You can read them directly, copy them, or version them with git. The files are plain markdown and stay in sync with your KB — every ingestion or group operation rewrites the relevant file.
