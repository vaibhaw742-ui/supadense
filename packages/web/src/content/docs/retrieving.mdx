Supadense gives you two ways to access your knowledge: the **chat interface** for natural language questions, and the **wiki** for structured browsing.

---

## Chat

Ask the agent anything and it will answer using only your KB:

```
What are the key differences between RAG and fine-tuning?
How does tool use work in LLM agents?
What did Karpathy say about tokenization?
```

The agent uses `kb_retrieve` internally to search your placements and construct an answer grounded in your sources. It will never answer from general training knowledge — only from what you've added.

### How retrieval works

1. Your question is embedded and matched against all placement content in the database
2. The most relevant placements are returned (ranked by semantic similarity)
3. The agent synthesizes an answer and cites which resources it drew from

### Tips for better retrieval

- Ask specific questions rather than broad ones
- Reference category names to scope the search: *"In the context of RAG, what is..."*
- If an answer seems thin, it means you haven't added enough resources on that topic yet — use `/memorize` to add more

## Wiki

The wiki at `/wiki` gives you a structured view of your entire KB.

### Navigation

- **Left sidebar** — your category tree. Click a category to open its wiki page
- **Section tabs** — switch between `overview`, `key-concepts`, and any custom sections
- **Subcategories** — nested under their parent in the sidebar

### Key concepts view

The `key-concepts` tab shows all extracted concepts from every resource placed in that category. If you've run `/group`, concepts are organized into named clusters — otherwise they appear as a flat list per resource.

### Sources panel

The right panel shows all resources that contributed to this category. Each source links back to the original URL.

### Images panel

Diagrams and visuals extracted from resources appear here. This is particularly useful for architecture diagrams, benchmark charts, and visual explanations.

---

## Roadmaps

Structured learning paths live under `/wiki/roadmap`. The agent builds these based on your goals and depth settings. Each roadmap gives you a curated sequence of topics to work through.

---

## Filtering

In the wiki you can filter the key-concepts view by:

- **Resource** — show only concepts from a specific source
- **Section** — view one section at a time
- **Group** — if groups are applied, filter to a specific conceptual cluster

See [Sources & Images](/docs/sources) for more on the sources and images panels.
